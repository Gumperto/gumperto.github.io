It is Monday, again. The clock ticks 25:00, again. My job is to open the cash register, count up the store assets, close the register, repeat until time reaches 4:00. Occasionally, a customer enters through the sliding glass, asks for some Eridanus brand, supervoid flavoured candy bar (which is not all that far from its previous void flavoured iteration, just super), or Europa brand, perpetually cold, refreshing can of vacuum-washed soda, and my job would then be to guide them to the right aisle. It is a menial job, but it pays my bills nonetheless.

1:00:

A special customer walked through at this time, whom I was told by the flat bright display to be a store regular. I believe it is appropriate to call such a “person”, loosely speaking anyways, a “you”, and as “they” when not referred to in direct conversation, for “they” have the appearance of one who came from Homeworld. But then again, anyone can look like anyone, albeit at a hefty price. Hazard a guess I did, and “they” did not seem to mind. What a relief. “They” asked me where to acquire a device resembling that of the store display, to which I responded such a thing was not for sale. “Their” frontal biological interface contorted in ways I could not describe, before “they” excused “themself” from the store. Peculiar fellows, Homeworlders are.
The day from then on was uneventful.

---

It is Tuesday, again. The clock ticks 25:00, again. My job is to open the cash register, count up the store assets, close the register, repeat until time reaches 4:00.

2:15:
Someone walked in, never before recorded in our logs. Most probably a Frontier 2 dweller, for I am most unfamiliar with its inhabitants. Friendly enough, though, for a person of a recently re-acquired world. “They”, as this customer wished to be called, told me of the Homeworlders’ acquisition of “their” home. “They” spoke of “their” ecstasy, of how “they” had always admired the Homeworlders’ tenacity, and their “unyielding determination to reclaim rightful unity despite all odds”. “They” spoke especially highly of “infallible ideologies”, of an empire impervious to “titillating, primitive proclamations of love for simple-minded isolation”. It echoed thoroughly the sentiments present in the textbooks we were taught back in Homeworld Values Education. Before long, “they” finished, bid me and the store farewell, having left without making any transactions.
The day from then on was uneventful.

---

It is Wednesday, again. The clock ticks 25:00, again. My job is to open the cash register, count up the store assets, close the register, repeat until time reaches 4:00.
And that I did. The cash register was opened a total of 26 times.
There were no visitors that day.

---

It is Thursday, again. The clock ticks 25:00, again. My job is to open the cash register, count up the store assets, close the register, repeat until time reaches 4:00.

1:40:

A strange customer previously encountered eight times stumbled into the store today. “Their” features resembled that of a particularly youthful Homeworlder, modified to have protuberances with the appearance of solid water growing out the back. Though, again, I couldn’t be sure, there were obvious works of biological engineering, after all. Nevertheless, it was none of my business. “They”, presumably a “they” at least, went up to the counter and asked for some lactic products. I inquired which brand “they” wanted it in, to which I received an answer I could not quite make out. I then decided to, at random, pick out whatever the store had, and handed it to the impatient-looking individual. “They” held onto it, stared at it like “they” were in a great deal of confusion. Yet, it appears to have met “their” needs, despite the much apparent bemusement. Upon leaving, carton in hand, “they” threw on the counter a number of circular metallic objects, which I assumed to be an esoteric currency from one of Homeworld’s many obscure nations. These hold no value on this planet, but I kept them there regardless. An unusual act for what may have been a Homeworlder.

3:50:

Just as I was about to leave the shift, someone else came to the store. A lively night of activity, I thought. They were a Homeworlder, through and through: clad in signature Homeworld military uniform, heavy suppression weaponry slung over their back. They left only the "face", as it is called, exposed, visual processing unit surveying the premises. The unit was pointed at the counter for one short moment, before they turned to leave through the front door, without even saying a single word.
I closed up the store shortly after that.

---

It is Friday, again. The clock ticks 25:00, again. My job is to open the cash register, count up the store assets, close the register, repeat until time reaches 4:00.

3:00

One "Qu'Phelia" entered the establishment today. A local living nearby, rhe comes here every Friday, always at this time, always for a pack of that rubbery substance… I remember it was called something like "gum". Always the kind, patient customer, not to mention always here during my shift, it didn't take long for me to remember who rhe was. As rhe walked in, I already had the usual order prepared, but instead of leaving like usual, rhe opted to linger, just a while longer. Rhe complained about the air getting chilly out, and so rhe wanted to take refuge inside the warm walls within.

3:30:

Having sensed the cold weather's unwillingness to submit, rhe, just slightly disgruntled, made for the exit, extending to me a word of gratitude before doing so. Hopefully that’s the only customer tonight.

4:00:

My shift was finished. I closed down the store and made my way home.

4:30:

Home. That old geezer spoke frankly. It is cold out today.

8:00:

The sky is pitch black. Everyone seems to be very confused.

12:30:

It should be noon. The sky still hums with silence, its blackness unchanging. It has gotten colder yet.

---

It is Saturday, again. I did not go to work.
Everyone is woefully unprepared. The store is not safe. The darkness still stretches as far as the eye can see. Astronomers say the field of stars that used to hang above cannot even be seen. It has gotten colder yet.
The streets fill with maddened marches.

---

It is Sunday, again. I did not go to work. It was not a work day, anyways.
The crowds dissipated. There was nothing left on the streets to scavenge. The darkness still stretches as far as the eye can see. Astronomers say the field of stars that used to hang above cannot even be seen. It has gotten colder yet.

---

It is Monday, again. I could not go to work.
We have been informed it was an extraterrestrial attack. Our access to sunlight has been completely blocked off. There was nothing we could do. Our planet lies on the border of Homeworld’s influence, and they have deemed us unworthy.



Homeworld has abandoned us.
