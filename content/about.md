## Work
I am a Vietnamese international undergraduate student based in Tokyo, Japan,
currently admitted as a student at Waseda University's School of Fundamental
Science and Engineering under their English program in [Computer Science and Communications Engineering](https://www.fse.sci.waseda.ac.jp/dept/csce/). 
I can somewhat comfortably support myself in Tokyo thanks to a [generous private scholarship program](https://www.fastretailing-foundation.or.jp/eng/scholarships/) 
which covers my tuition and gives me a quarterly stipend.
My own financial conscientiousness goes the rest of the way.

I have co-authored a [paper about probing evaluation awareness in LLMs](https://openreview.net/forum?id=lerUefpec2), 
which was cited in [Bengio et. al's International AI Safety Report 2026](https://internationalaisafetyreport.org/publication/international-ai-safety-report-2026).
I worked as and/or was otherwise associated with roles like: co-organizer 
for [Hanoi AI Safety Network](https://ai-alignment-and-rationality.github.io/)  (currently inactive),
research fellow at [antoan.ai](https://www.antoan.ai/), 
research fellow at [Shibaura Institute of Technology](https://www.shibaura-it.ac.jp/en/).

If you need to reach out, my contact information can be found in the [Links page](/links).

Unless specified otherwise, the views expressed in [gumperto.github.io](/) are my own.

## Personality
By my own standards I would not describe myself as being particularly curious nor
agentic. I have not taken any proper assessments of this, but I cannot 
count how many skills, languages, fields of knowledge, activities, etc, 
I lament not having gotten a headstart on from, say, middle school. I 
would say this should make me much more wary of [hyperbolic discounting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyperbolic_discounting), 
though I feel basically fine at the time of writing. This is probably 
a bad sign for future me when I have to update this laundry list of regrets.

The saving grace here is the part of my personality that gets easily excited about stuff I'm consuming, 
which I then proceed to be unreasonably gung-ho about trying to do it myself whilst
vastly underestimating the difficulty. This has generally always been a good 
decision. And luckily for me, it has become comparatively much easier than it was 
in my parents' time to hijack my brain into learning hard new things in 
the vastness of a world with things like the Internet and personal computers.   

Learning Japanese on a two-year-to-fluency timeframe out of a desire to
have the best possible Comiket/Reitaisai experience seems slightly out-of-reach
given current progress, but ironically I feel much more capable in many ways than
what I had expected was possible ~10 months ago.

Something similar happened many years ago when I saw a friend do magic CLI tricks 
on their Ubuntu system (it was just `sl`; I was easy to impress :P). Using a 
Unix-based system where I came from at the time was really not as trivial 
as it is today, so I'm glad that my hours flailing around `rm -rf`-ing my 
home directories (this really did happen) led me to appreciating computers 
~~that do not run Microsoft Windows~~ as much as I do now.

This site is something similar to that, on the level of "I'm a big fan of
the idea of blogging about stuff you work on, but you have to actually work
on stuff to have content you can blog about, so I'm hoping this would offshoot
into a bunch of cool projects". Yeah, I'm that kind of person.
